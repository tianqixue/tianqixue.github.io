I am a PhD candidate in Quantitative Marketing at the Chinese University of Hong Kong.

My research focuses on empirical marketing. I prefer to select the most appropriate methodology to address each research question, including causal inference, structural modeling, Bayesian statistics, and machine learning.

I maintain close collaborations with firms across a range of sectors, including high-tech, e-commerce, traditional retail, and gaming, often involving proprietary data and empirical work. I also support these firms in managerial decision making, including demand estimation, pricing and promotion strategies, product design, and the evaluation of marketing interventions.
